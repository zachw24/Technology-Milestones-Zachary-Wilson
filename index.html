LED Display

This is a story about LED Display Overall, A LED display is a flat panel display that uses an array of light-emitting diodes as pixels for a video display. The LED diodes came into existence in 1962 and were in primary color of red for the first decade that they were in use. Nick Holonyak, Jr. invented the first particle. He invented it in 1962. The first commercial use practical was invented by Hewlett-Packard in 1968. One year later, it was incorporated into the HP’s display and it became a digital Display. This became the basis for LED displays for the coming future. The efficient blue LED was not completed until the late 1980 is which resulted in a more efficient cost. In the late 1980s, Aluminum Indium Gallium Phosphide LEDs arrived. This led to a more efficient source of red and amber, which were used in informational displays. Throughout this time, full color still was not possible. Green, Blue, and Yellow LED has needed a drastic improvement. This did not happen until Shuji Nakumura announced the new changes, which opened new possibilities to the LED video displays. Overall, if it was not for Shuji improvements throughout this time we would not have what we have today for big LED screens in churches, gaming stadiums, and even sports stadiums.

Moving to the 1990’s, Samsung was the first cooperation to release a smartwatch to the overall public with calling capabilities built into it. They were the first to introduce these technology capabilities to the overall public as well as the android Market. A quarter century later during the 2010’s. Pebble, a Kickstarter project, was the first overall major company who took full control of the smartwatch and made it into what it can do today. In 2012 Pebble designed the independent watch that could make phone calls, use maps, and take full advantage of apps on the android watch. This began the new trend or new age of wearable technology. In 2013 Sony as well as Samsung released their very own smartwatches. Following the low impact that they made Apple jumped into play with their very own smart watch in 2015. They sold over 11 million pieces. Leading to this apple created new technology in the wearable watch that can now act as a phone without your phone being around with cellular data. Overall, during this current time period Samsung, Fitbit, and apple are all battling over the crown of the smartwatch.
